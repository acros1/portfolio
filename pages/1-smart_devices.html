Smart devices

Supervized by Jérémie Grisolia
This module objective is to complete a thorough process and be able to design and build a smart device based on the combination of a gas sensor and an electronic card to communicate data over a low speed network.
  • Desriptive part
  • Technical part
  • Analytic part

As an ISS engineer, it is very important to understand how a smart device works at a low-level. In this way, this course is divided in three parts. The first is the complete creation of a gas sensor from a silicon wafer and using the microprocessor manufacturing methods. The second one is to study the analog electronic part of a smart device. In the case of the gas sensor, the goal is to manage the resistance values to keep only useful information. Finally, the third part represent the data exchange of the smart device and its ability to send measurement over a network.

Gas sensor creation

The gas sensor manufacturing is based on the microprocessor’s creation method. It starts from a silicon wafer on which we need to deposit each material on the entire plate [image]. For every material we keep only the necessary surface by removing the rest using chemical attacks. The second manipulation is to put the nano-sensor on a support and weld its terminal to the support’s connectors. As each cable is as thick as a hair, we use ultrasonic welding methods [image]. The next step is to chemically create conductive tungsten nanoparticles in order to stick them on the aluminium component to create the resistance [image]. The last part is to create the sensor datasheet after realizing calibration measurement before being able to distribute the sensor.
