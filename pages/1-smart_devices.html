Smart devices

Supervized by Jérémie Grisolia
This module objective is to complete a thorough process and be able to design and build a smart device based on the combination of a gas sensor and an electronic card to communicate data over a low speed network.
  • Desriptive part
  • Technical part
  • Analytic part

As an ISS engineer, it is very important to understand how a smart device works at a low-level. In this way, this course is divided in three parts. The first is the complete creation of a gas sensor from a silicon wafer and using the microprocessor manufacturing methods.
The second one is to study the analog electronic part of a smart device. In the case of the gas sensor, the goal is to manage the resistance values to keep only useful information.
Finally, the third part represent the data exchange of the smart device and its ability to send measurement over a network.

Gas sensor creation

The gas sensor manufacturing is based on the microprocessor’s creation method. It starts from a silicon wafer on which we need to deposit each material on the entire plate (cf Fig. 1). For every material we keep only the necessary surface by removing the rest using chemical attacks.
The second manipulation is to put the nano-sensor on a support and weld its terminal to the support’s connectors. As each cable is as thick as a hair, we use ultrasonic welding methods (cf Fig. 2).
The next step is to chemically create conductive tungsten nanoparticles in order to stick them on the aluminium component to create the resistance (cf Fig. 3).
The last part is to create the sensor datasheet after realizing calibration measurement before being able to distribute the sensor.

Analog electronic analysis

In this part we used LTSpice software in order to simulate the entire analog electronic part of the smart device module (cf Fig. 4).

By using this method, we are able to design filters and electronic treatments in order to retrieve only the useful information of the gas sensor.

Smart device communication

This part allows us to implement the complete smart device gathering a gas sensor and an Arduino board (cf Fig. 5). The goal is to send the sensor measurements over a LoRa network.
We also add to weld the microchip on the board in order to get a functional LoRa module.

By using this method, we are able to design filters and electronic treatments in order to retrieve only the useful information of the gas sensor.

On a global point of view this module is very interesting regarding my background. As my formation dealt much with the high-level of IT systems, it is very rewarding to concentrate on low-level. Moreover, the ability to access to the micro and nano-electronics labs of INSA Toulouse is a real opportunity. Work on rare and expensive components including a scanning electron microscope (Jeol JSM-7800f) is a nice experience.
Finally, this idea of building a smart device from beginning to the end will be a real asset as an engineer. I have now the possibility to debate, discuss and have a well-informed eye on a complete device development even if I was not able to totally understand every step of the process (chemical and electronical parts for instance).
