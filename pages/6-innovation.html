Innovation and Humanity

Supervized by Hélène Hereng
The human aspect is a big part in the engineer’s work. Gain knowledges concerning management, human psychology and creativity is not only the key for and IoT engineer but also for every life aspect.
  • Desriptive part
  • Technical part
  • Analytic part

As the physical activity has been removed from this module due to the pandemic, it is composed of three parts. The two first part gather skills about innovation, creativity methods and business development. The second one is sharing competencies on team management while the last part is studying social acceptability and human psychology.

Social acceptability

This practical part is oriented around the film 12 Angry Men. The purpose is to analyse how does twelve men are judging a young person. The are several key points as the behaviour of one man who is not agree with the eleven others. Moreover, the group effect is important to examine because at the end, they all agreed with the one who have a different point of view.
At the end we synthetized some document in order to achieve a lecture about psychosocial risks and workplace happiness.

Creativity methods

In this part we used the TRIZ methods in order to analyse the innovation process of the glasses. The TRIZ theory is centred around problem resolution. The goal is to resolve different technical contradictions to anticipate and forecast the next innovation step on the product.

The Innovation and Humanity module is one of the major courses that as nothing to deal with the IT technical part. However, it is very crucial for an engineer to be versatile and to be able to manage people, to understand innovation methods and to have competencies regarding the human behaviour comprehension. In my point of view a good engineer is a person who has a lot of skills concerning technical concept but above all, an engineer must be mature and be able to always self-analyse in order to improve his human aspects.
